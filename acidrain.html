Consequences of Air Pollution
Air pollution threatens the health of humans and other living begins on our planet. It causes acid rain and depletion of the ozone layer contributing to global warming. Industrial plumes contain sulphur dioxide and oxides of nitrogen. These gases convert to acids such as sulphuric acid and nitric acid . Nitric acid causes acid deposition that can damage trees and marine ecosystems due to a decrease in the pH of soil and water. These acids may cause dry or wet deposition on both land and water. The deposition of acids can damage the natural surface water system as well as the grasslands,forests and other vegetation.
